# Budget and cash flow (reference)

## Fixed versus variable expenses

Fixed expenses are amounts that typically stay the same month to month, such as rent and some subscriptions. Variable expenses change with usage, such as groceries and dining out. Separating them helps you see where you have flexibility if you need to adjust spending.

## Cash flow and the 50/30/20 idea

A common budgeting framework allocates after-tax income into needs, wants, and savings/ debt. Percentages are illustrative; your real split depends on your situation. The point is to align spending with priorities and avoid chronic negative cash flow.

## Tracking spending

Tracking does not mean judging every purchase. It means noticing patterns: where money goes, when recurring charges creep, and whether spending matches your values. Many people review weekly or monthly rather than daily.
