# Credit management (reference)

## Credit scores and reports

Credit scores summarize information from credit reports. Factors often include payment history, amounts owed, length of history, credit mix, and new credit. Checking your own report can help you spot errors; you can learn about dispute processes from official consumer guidance.

## APR and the minimum payment

APR is annualized interest on revolving balances. Paying only the minimum can keep debt outstanding for a long time and increase total interest. Many people use a payoff strategy such as paying higher interest balances first or focusing on smaller balances for motivation.

## Where to find APR, balance, and minimum payment

On a credit card, the APR, your current balance, and the minimum payment are usually shown on your billing statement and/or in your online account app. Look for headings like “Summary of Account,” “Current Balance,” “Minimum Payment,” and “Interest Rate” or “APR.” Educationally, the exact labels vary by issuer, so use the statement or account app you have and note whatever numbers you see.

## Utilization and payment timing

Credit utilization is how much of your revolving limits you use. Lower utilization is often associated with better scores, but utilization is a broad educational concept, not a guarantee about any individual score. Paying on time consistently matters for avoiding late fees and negative marks.
