# Workplace retirement plans (reference)

## Employer match basics

Some employers match a portion of employee contributions to a workplace retirement plan. Match formulas vary widely. "Free money" is a common phrase; the educational point is to read your plan summary for the formula, limits, and eligibility rules.

## Where to find employer match and contribution rate

Your employer usually provides the 401(k) match details through a benefits portal, an enrollment/plan summary document, or an offer letter package. Common places include a “Plan Details” page, “Summary Plan Description,” or a matching worksheet during enrollment. For your current contribution rate, check your 401(k) enrollment screen for the percentage you are contributing.

## Contribution rates and limits

Contributions are often expressed as a percentage of pay. Annual contribution limits and tax treatment depend on plan rules and tax law. If you need limits for your situation, consult official IRS materials or a qualified professional; this text is general education only.

## Vesting

Vesting determines when employer contributions become yours if you leave the employer. Cliff schedules and graded schedules are common patterns. Your plan document should describe what applies to you.
