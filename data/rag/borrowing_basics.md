# Borrowing basics (reference)

## Secured versus unsecured debt

Secured debt is tied to collateral; unsecured debt is not. Collateral can be repossessed in default scenarios depending on contract and law. This is general education, not legal advice.

## Interest rates and amortization

Interest is the cost of borrowing. Amortization describes how payments split between principal and interest over time. Longer terms can lower monthly payments but increase total interest if other terms are equal.

## Debt-to-income as a planning lens

Debt-to-income compares monthly debt payments to monthly gross income. Lenders use it in underwriting; individuals sometimes use it to gauge whether new borrowing is reasonable. Thresholds vary by context; treat numbers as illustrative.
