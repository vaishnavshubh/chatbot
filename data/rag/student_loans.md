# Student loans (reference)

## Federal versus private loans

Federal loans often have standardized repayment options and protections described in official loan materials. Private loans are contracts with the lender and can differ in rates, fees, and flexibility. Comparing terms using disclosures is a general skill, not a recommendation of any lender.

## Repayment plans

Repayment plans may include standard, graduated, extended, or income-driven options for some federal loans. Each plan changes monthly payment, total interest, and time in repayment. Trade-offs are situational; use official loan servicer tools for estimates.

## Where to find loan principal, interest rate, and monthly payment

Student loan statements and servicer dashboards typically show your outstanding principal (the remaining balance), the interest rate for each loan (or an average), and your current monthly payment amount. For federal loans, your official student portal usually lists these details across loans and repayment options. Educationally, labels can vary, so use the headings for “Current Balance/Principal,” “Interest Rate,” and “Monthly Payment” and copy the values you see.

## Extra payments and refinancing concepts

Paying extra toward principal can reduce interest over time. Refinancing replaces a loan with a new loan and may change rate, term, and protections. Refinancing federal loans into private loans can lose federal protections; that is a decision framework topic, not advice for your case.
