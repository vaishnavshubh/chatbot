# Financial foundations (reference)

## Emergency funds and cash buffers

An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses or income loss. Many people aim for a starter amount (for example, one month of essential expenses) and then build toward a larger cushion over time. The right target depends on job stability, dependents, and how quickly you could replace income. This is educational framing only; it is not a personal recommendation.

## Net worth and financial goals

Net worth is assets minus liabilities. Tracking it occasionally can help you see progress over time. Financial goals often mix short-term (under a year), medium-term (one to a few years), and long-term (years to decades). Writing goals in specific, measurable terms tends to make planning easier than vague intentions.

## Paying yourself first

"Pay yourself first" means automating savings or debt payments before discretionary spending when possible. Automation reduces the willpower burden. Many people use separate savings accounts or split direct deposit to keep the money out of sight.
